#### [Wikipedia: Foe (unit)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foe_(unit))

> A foe is a unit of energy equal to 10<sup>44</sup> joules or 10<sup>51</sup> ergs, used to express the large amount of energy released by a supernova. An acronym for "[ten to the power of] fifty-one ergs", the term was introduced by Gerald E. Brown of Stony Brook University in his work with Hans Bethe, because "it came up often enough in our work".
